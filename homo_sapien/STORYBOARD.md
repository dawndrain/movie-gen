# "Homo Sapien" — Lenka — music video storyboard

**Runtime:** 3:59 (239s) — cut to Lenka's original master (`audio/homo_sapien.m4a`).
**Timed lyrics:** `lyrics_timed.md` (whisper word-timestamps on the actual master, lyrics hand-corrected).

---

## The concept

**"The Same Two."**

Every stage of the universe is *them*. Two sparks off the Big Bang, two stars in a binary,
two blobs in the tidepool, two fish, two monkeys, two hominids, two people in a small
kitchen at 1am — the same pair of souls, reincarnating up the tree of life, always
finding each other, always a little bit useless at it, always in love.

The song is sweet and kooky, so the video is sincere about the love and silly about the
evolution. Nothing is grand or David-Attenborough-solemn: the primordial slime is a
tidepool the size of a soup bowl, the hominids are bad at hunting, the Victorian portrait
gets ruined because they can't stop laughing. The cosmic scale is the joke; the couple is
the point.

### Three motifs that carry the whole thing

1. **The forehead touch.** Every incarnation does it — fish nudging snouts, monkeys
   bonking heads, cave couple, modern couple, old couple. It's the through-line the
   audience learns without being told. Repeat the *staging*, not the clip.
2. **Warm + cool.** One of the pair always reads coral/amber, the other teal/blue. Two
   sparks, two stars, two blobs, two fish, her sweater / his shirt. It's how you know
   which two out of a crowd are "them" in a half-second cut.
3. **Two handprints.** Pressed in ochre on a cave wall at 1:36 → behind museum glass at
   3:22 → in kid's poster paint on the fridge in the final shot.

### Look

Warm, tactile, handmade — natural-history-diorama meets storybook. Golden practical light,
shallow depth, soft grain. Nothing chrome, nothing clinical, no CGI-blue sci-fi. Cosmos
sequences are painterly and hand-mixed rather than Hubble-photoreal, so they sit in the
same world as the kitchen.

## Shot list

Format: `# | in–out (dur) | lyric | shot`. **Gen dur** = what to generate (longer than the

### COLD OPEN — 0:00–0:17.5 (kitchen, no vocal)

**S01 · 0:00–0:07.0 (7.0s)** — *[intro, ukulele + claps]*
Small kitchen, 1am. Kettle steaming. **May** alone, barefoot on lino, doing a private
terrible little dance while she waits for the tea. Locked-off static camera. Warm tungsten,
one window of blue night behind her.
*Gen 9s.*

**S02 · 0:07.0–0:13.0 (6.0s)**
**Ollie** shuffles in half-asleep, sees her, joins the terrible dance without comment. They
hold each other and sway, badly, out of time. Static camera. This is the whole film in six
seconds — establish the forehead touch here.
*Gen 8s.*

**S03 · 0:13.0–0:17.5 (4.5s)**
Slow push into May's eye as she laughs. Reflection in the iris: the blue of the window
becomes a starfield. Hold on black for the last beat before the vocal.
*Gen 6s, trim to land the flash exactly on "First".*

### VERSE 1 — 0:17.5–1:00.5 (the cosmos and the slime)

**S04 · 0:17.5–0:21.5 (4.0s)** — *"First there was the big bang"*
White bloom out of nothing. Matter unfurls in warm dust. Out of the flash, **two sparks** —
one coral, one teal — spin away together, already orbiting. Kooky, not grand: the Big Bang
looks like glitter in a shaft of light.
*Gen 6s. Cut hard on "bang".*

**S05 · 0:21.5–0:25.6 (4.1s)** — *"and stars began to shine"*
Two stars in a binary, orbiting each other in a painterly nebula, close enough to nearly
touch — a forehead touch at cosmic scale. Everything else in frame is empty.
*Gen 6s.*

**S06 · 0:25.6–0:34.9 (9.3s)** — *"Then there was existence, primordial slime"*
A tidepool the size of a soup bowl on a black volcanic rock. Macro. Two glowing
single-celled blobs — coral and teal — wobble toward each other through the murk, bump,
recoil, bump again. Comic. They stick.
*Gen 10s.*

**S07 · 0:34.9–0:42.8 (7.9s)** — *"How I was waiting all that time"*
One small creature alone on a wet rock, chin (such as it has) on its stub-fin, **waiting**.
Behind it a geological time-lapse rips past — tides, ice, continents, a hundred million
years of weather — and it doesn't move once. Deadpan patience. Locked-off static camera.
*Gen 9s. The single funniest shot in the video; give it the room.*

**S08 · 0:42.8–0:51.5 (8.7s)** — *"While we were evolving side by side"*
Two parallel lanes of evolution running side by side like two conveyor belts, coral on the
left, teal on the right — each creature handing off to the next, always glancing sideways
at the other lane to check the other one is keeping up.
*Gen 10s.*

**S09a · 0:51.5–0:53.5 (2.0s)** — *"Rock to fish…"*
Two fish, coral and teal, hanging in green light, fins touching, snouts nudging.
*Gen 5s, trim to 2s.*

**S09b · 0:53.5–0:55.5 (2.0s)** — *"…monkey to man"*
Two monkeys hanging upside-down by their tails from the same branch, holding hands,
bonking foreheads.
*Gen 5s, trim to 2s. Beat-cut on "monkey" / "man".*

**S10 · 0:55.5–1:00.5 (5.0s)** — *"It's why this love begun"*
Savanna at dawn. Two hominids, silhouetted, forehead-to-forehead. The gesture, third time,
now unmistakable. Camera slowly rises. Golden.
*Gen 7s.*

### VERSE 2 — 1:43.8–2:08.5 (fire and mutation)

**S20 · 1:43.8–1:51.7 (7.9s)** — *"We formed and mutated over time"*
Two mud-and-clay figures pulling themselves into shape out of a riverbank, sculpting

**S21 · 1:51.7–2:00.7 (9.0s)** — *"We hunted and gathered, and played with fire"*

## Production notes (what's different because we're using her real audio)

- **No dialogue, no singing, no lip-sync, ever.** Every clip prompt must carry:
  *"Nobody speaks and nobody sings; no lips move; there is no dialogue and no voiceover."*
  Otherwise Seedance will grow spontaneous speech and it will fight the vocal.
  **Mute 100% of clip audio at assembly** (`volume=0` on every entry) and lay
  `audio/homo_sapien.m4a` over the finished picture as a single master track.
- **Therefore: no voice pipeline, no TTS, no ElevenLabs, no dubbing, no whisper-QC.** The
  entire back half of MOVIE_LESSONS.md is irrelevant here. Likewise Sonilo — we have the song.
- **Cuts must land on the beat, not near it.** Generate everything ~2s long and trim into
  the exact lyric window in the assembler. `lyrics_timed.md` has the frame-accurate starts.
- **The assembler becomes an edit, not a concat.** Build the CUT list against absolute
  timeline positions from `lyrics_timed.md`, not by summing durations — one drifted clip
  otherwise pushes every subsequent cut off the beat.
- **Still applies from the playbook:** anchors → start frames → 480p `--std` clips (never
  fast); restate the full wardrobe lock verbatim in *every* prompt; "locked-off static
  camera, no zoom, no pan" on every shot that isn't S03/S27/S32/S37/S40; the 8-job Seedance
  cap; keep every take (versioned `outputs/videoN/`); never retake a 95% shot.
- **Cheap wins available:** S12/S14 are match cuts of S11/S13 — shoot them with the same
  framing and the edit does the work. S38 is S02 re-staged. The museum dioramas (S35) reuse
  creature anchors already paid for.
