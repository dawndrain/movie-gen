# BURNT NJAL ŌĆö storyboard v1

~40 shots, target Ōēł 6ŌĆō7 min. Source: Dasent translation (`njal_dasent.txt`); all quoted
dialogue is verbatim and lip-syncable. See `SOURCE_NOTES.md` for the full dialogue bank,
character bible, and content warnings.

**Adaptation choice**: the film is the tragedy triangle ŌĆö Gunnar's fall (Act I), the murder
of Hoskuld (Act II), the Burning and its healing (Act III). The Hrut/Gunnhilda prologue
generation survives only as the cold-open prophecy. Clontarf, the conversion, and the
lawsuit are compressed to single beats. Njal's beardlessness, the slap, and Thrain's
jaw-tooth are the three planted props that pay off.

## PROLOGUE ŌĆö THE PROPHECY (2 shots)

**p1_thiefs_eyes** (12s) ŌĆö Firelit feast hall, Hauskuldstede. Child Hallgerda (long silk-fair
hair to her waist) plays on the floor. Hauskuld calls her: "Come hither to me, daughter."
Kisses her brow, turns to his brother: "Is she not fair?" HRUT, grave, after a silence:
"Fair enough is this maid, and many will smart for it; but this I know not, whence thief's
eyes have come into our race." Close on the child's eyes. HOLD.

**p2_title** (6s) ŌĆö Slow push over black-sand coast and green dales. On-screen text baked
into Nano Banana start frame: **BURNT NJAL** ŌĆö *Iceland, in the days of the Althing*.
Music cue M1 (omen motif).

## ACT I ŌĆö GUNNAR AND HALLGERDA (12 shots)

**a1_wooing** (12s) ŌĆö The Althing, sunlit booths. Gunnar (scarlet finery) meets grown
HALLGERDA. G: "How wouldst thou answer were I to ask for thee?" H: "That can not be in thy
mind." G: "It is though."

**a2_njal_warning** (12s) ŌĆö Bergthorsknoll interior. NJAL: "From her will arise all kind of
ill if she comes hither east." GUNNAR: "Never shall she spoil our friendship." NJAL, quiet:
"Thou wilt have always to make atonement for her."

**a3_feast_quarrel** (14s) ŌĆö Feast at Bergthorsknoll. Seat dispute. HALLGERDA: "There's not
much to choose between you two. Thou hast hangnails on every finger, and Njal is beardless."
BERGTHORA: "But Thorwald, thy husband, was not beardless, and yet thou plottedst his death."
HALLGERDA, to Gunnar: "It stands me in little stead to have the bravest man in Iceland if
thou dost not avenge this!" GUNNAR, rising: "Home I will go ŌĆö never will I be egged on by
thee like a fool."

**a4_feud_montage** (12s, mute + M2 under) ŌĆö The wives' proxy war: a man axed at a charcoal
pit; another speared in a wood; the SAME purse of silver passed GunnarŌåÆNjal, then NjalŌåÆGunnar,
their handclasp each time. End on SKARPHEDINN's grin: "Hallgerda does not let our house-carles
die of old age."

**a5_slap** (12s) ŌĆö Lithend table. Gunnar realizes the food is stolen: "Ill indeed is it if I
am a partaker with thieves." His hand flies up ŌĆö CUT to her cheek already turned, her hand
rising to it (impact off-frame). HALLGERDA, cold: "I will bear that slap in mind, and repay it
if I can."

**a6_two_conditions** (14s) ŌĆö Njal and Gunnar alone on the green. NJAL: "Never slay more than
one man in the same stock, and never break the peace which good men make between thee and
others ŌĆö else thou wilt have but a little while to live; but otherwise thou wilt come to be
an old man." GUNNAR: "Dost thou know what will be thine own death?" NJAL: "I know it." G:
"What?" NJAL, looking away: "That which all would be the last to think."

**a7_ford_fight** (10s) ŌĆö Rangriver ford. Gunnar alone vs. eight riders; the bill SINGS
(ringing metallic note). Fast brutal choreography, spear caught mid-air and hurled back.
After, to Kolskegg, quiet: "I would like to know whether I am by so much the less brisk and
bold than other men, because I think more of killing men than they?"

**a8_outlawry** (8s) ŌĆö Hill of Laws, crowd. GIZUR proclaims: Gunnar shall fare abroad three
winters ŌĆö "but if he does not fare, then he may be slain." Njal watches, grim.

**a9_fair_lithe** (12s) ŌĆö THE pivot. Riding to the ship, Gunnar's horse stumbles; he springs
off, gaze caught upslope. GUNNAR: "Fair is the Lithe; so fair that it has never seemed to me
so fair; the corn fields are white to harvest, and the home mead is mown; and now I will ride
back home, and not fare abroad at all." KOLSKEGG: "Tell my kinsmen and my mother I never mean
to see Iceland again ŌĆö for I shall soon learn that thou art dead, brother." They part.
Music M3 (elegy) starts.

**a10_siege_begins** (12s) ŌĆö Night, Lithend. Forty shadowed men in the sunken road. The hound
Sam's great dying howl. Gunnar wakes in the loft: "Thou hast been sorely treated, Sam, my
fosterling ŌĆö this warning is so meant that our two deaths will not be far apart." Outside, a
climber peers in the window-slit; the bill flashes out; he walks back and falls: GIZUR: "Well,
is Gunnar at home?" THORGRIM: "Find that out for yourselves; but this I am sure of ŌĆö his bill
is at home." Falls dead.

**a11_bowstring** (15s) ŌĆö Interior loft, arrows nearly spent, bowstring cut. GUNNAR: "Give me
two locks of thy hair, and ye two, my mother and thou, twist them together into a bowstring
for me." HALLGERDA: "Does aught lie on it?" GUNNAR: "My life lies on it." HALLGERDA: "Then I
call to thy mind that slap on the face which thou gavest me ŌĆö and I care never a whit whether
thou holdest out a long while or a short." GUNNAR, calm: "Every one has something to boast
of, and I will ask thee no more for this." RANNVEIG: "Thou behavest ill, and this shame shall
long be had in mind."

**a12_gunnar_falls** (10s) ŌĆö Exterior: the roof hauled off with ropes, a wall of attackers.
Gunnar's last stand shown as silhouette against the sky ŌĆö he falls (off-frame). GIZUR, over
the quiet yard: "We have now laid low to earth a mighty chief, and hard work has it been; and
the fame of this defence of his shall last as long as men live in this land." M3 resolves.

*(Optional alt: a12b_cairn ŌĆö moonlit open cairn, four shadowless lights, the dead man's song:
"Sooner die than yield an inch." Shoot if credits allow; great act-break.)*

## ACT II ŌĆö THE FOSTER-SON (10 shots)

**b1_ice_slide** (10s) ŌĆö Frozen Markfleet, blinding bright. Skarphedinn's shoe-thong snaps ŌĆö
"I am tying my shoe" ŌĆö then he LEAPS twelve ells over black water and glides standing across
glass-ice "as fast as a bird flies," axe falling once as he passes Thrain; jaw-teeth scatter
on the ice. He stoops and pockets ONE TOOTH (plant the prop). KARI: "This was done like a man."

**b2_gold_ring** (12s) ŌĆö Firelit evening. Old Njal shows the orphan boy Hoskuld a gold ring.
NJAL: "Knowest thou what brought thy father to his death?" BOY: "I know that Skarphedinn slew
him; but we need not keep that in mind, when an atonement has been made and a full price paid."
NJAL, moved: "Better answered than asked ŌĆö thou wilt live to be a good man and true." Embrace.

**b3_one_law** (8s) ŌĆö Hill of Laws, two armed factions roaring. THORGEIR THE LAWSPEAKER rises
from beneath his cloak: "If there be a sundering of the laws, then there will be a sundering
of the peace. This is the beginning of our laws: that all men shall be Christian here in the
land." Silence; crosses raised. (Establishes the Christian frame Njal dies inside.)

**b4_valgard_deathbed** (10s) ŌĆö Dark hall at Hof. Dying VALGARD to Mord: "Set them by the ears
by tale-bearing, so that Njal's sons may slay Hauskuld ŌĆö there are many who will have the
blood-feud after him, and so Njal's sons will be slain in that quarrel." He snaps a wooden
cross. Mord's thin smile.

**b5_slander** (14s) ŌĆö Two-hander pair of visits. MORD to Skarphedinn: "They gave thee a horse
which they called a 'dark horse' ŌĆö out of mockery at thee. They mean to fall upon you."
MORD to Hoskuld, oily. HOSKULD: "Thou canst never say so much ill of Njal's sons as to make me
believe it; and were it true, I would far rather suffer death at their hands than work them
any harm." (dramatic-irony hinge of the film)

**b6_hoskuld_slain** (12s) ŌĆö Ossaby, dawn, sun just risen. Hoskuld in the scarlet cloak sows
corn by the fence. Figures rise behind it. SKARPHEDINN: "Don't try to turn on thy heel,
Whiteness priest." The axe falls FROM BEHIND THE FENCE, off-frame; corn seed scatters across
the dirt. HOSKULD (V. weakening): "God help me, and forgive you!" Hold on spilled seed and
one edge of scarlet cloth. M1 (omen) sting.

**b7_njal_grief** (14s) ŌĆö Bergthorsknoll. NJAL: "Methinks it were better to have lost two of
my sons, and that Hauskuld lived ŌĆö the sweetest light of my eyes was quenched." SKARPHEDINN:
"What will come after?" NJAL: "My death, and the death of my wife, and of all my sons."
KARI: "What dost thou foretell for me?" NJAL: "They will have hard work to go against thy
good fortune, for thou wilt be more than a match for all of them."

**b8_bloody_cloak** (15s) ŌĆö Swinefell/Ossaby hall. Hildigunna serves Flosi in silence, then
lifts from her chest the stiff dark-stained cloak and THROWS it over his shoulders; dried
flakes rattle down. HILDIGUNNA: "This cloak, Flosi, thou gavest to Hauskuld, and now I give
it back to thee; he was slain in it. I call God and all good men to witness that I adjure
thee, by all the might of thy Christ, and by thy manhood and bravery: take vengeance for all
those wounds ŌĆö or else be called every man's dastard." FLOSI, face going red, then ash-pale:
"Thou art the greatest hell-hag. Women's counsel is ever cruel."

**b9_settlement_breaks** (15s) ŌĆö Court of Laws; a heaped pile of silver, a fine silken scarf
laid on top. FLOSI lifts the scarf, laughs: "Who may have given this? Thy father, the
Beardless Carle? ŌĆö for many who look at him know not whether he is more a man than a woman."
SKARPHEDINN: "He has had sons by his wife; and few of our kinsfolk lie unavenged by our
house." (hurls a pair of blue breeks at Flosi) "Thou wilt have more need of these."
FLOSI, kicking the silver: "Go we now home ŌĆö one fate shall befall us all."

**b10_oath** (8s) ŌĆö The Great Rift, dusk, 120 men. FLOSI: "We shall ride to Bergthorsknoll,
and fall on Njal's sons with fire and sword, and not turn away before they are all dead."
Hands clasped one over another down the line. M4 (dread drone) begins.

## ACT III ŌĆö THE BURNING (9 shots)

**c1_last_supper** (12s) ŌĆö Bergthorsknoll, evening table. BERGTHORA: "Now shall ye choose your
meat to-night, each what he likes best; for this evening is the last that I shall set meat
before my household." Njal stares past the fire, stricken. NJAL: "Methinks the gable wall is
thrown down, and the whole board, and the meat on it, is one gore of blood." Household frozen.

**c2_indoors** (14s) ŌĆö Yard, torches approaching across the dark meads. NJAL: "My will is that
our men go indoors ŌĆö they had hard work to master Gunnar of Lithend, and he was alone."
SKARPHEDINN: "These men will fall on us with fire ŌĆö I am unwilling to be stifled indoors like
a fox in his earth. But I may well humour my father in this, by being burnt indoors along with
him; for I am not afraid of my death." They file in. FLOSI, outside: "Now are they all fey,
since they have gone indoors."

**c3_fire** (12s) ŌĆö FLOSI to his men: "Now there are but two choices, and neither of them
good: to turn away ŌĆö and that is our death; or to set fire, and burn them inside; and that is
a deed we shall have to answer for heavily before God, since we are Christian men. But still
we must take to that counsel." The vetch-stack flares against the loft. SKARPHEDINN, from
within, grinning through a window-slit: "What, lads! Are ye lighting a fire, or are ye taking
to cooking?"

**c4_njal_refuses** (15s) ŌĆö Doorway, firelight. Women and children file out past the spears.
FLOSI: "I will offer thee, master Njal, leave to go out; it is unworthy that thou shouldst
burn indoors." NJAL: "I will not go out, for I am an old man, and little fitted to avenge my
sons; but I will not live in shame." BERGTHORA: "I was given away to Njal young, and I have
promised him this ŌĆö that we would both share the same fate." They turn back inside together.

**c5_the_bed** (14s) ŌĆö Interior, smoke gathering at the beams. The small grandson THORD:
"Thou hast promised me, grandmother, that we should never part so long as I wished to be with
thee; and methinks it is much better to die with thee and Njal than to live after you."
NJAL: "We will go to our bed and lay us down; I have long been eager for rest." The three lie
down; the ox-hide is drawn over them; hands sign the cross. CUT AWAY on the hide settling ŌĆö
nothing further shown. M4 swells.

**c6_kari_escape** (10s) ŌĆö The cross-beam under the broken roof, all smoke and embers.
KARI: "This parting of ours will be in such wise that we shall never see one another more."
SKARPHEDINN: "It joys me, brother-in-law, to think that if thou gettest away, thou wilt
avenge me." Kari runs the beam and leaps OUT through flame, clothes alight, rolls into the
stream ŌĆö steam, night, breath.

**c7_skarphedinn_end** (13s) ŌĆö Skarphedinn wedged at the gable, fire-glow. GUNNAR LAMBI'S SON,
atop the wall, laughing: "Weepest thou now, Skarphedinn?" SKARPHEDINN: "Not so ŌĆö the smoke
makes one's eyes smart. But is it as it seems to me ŌĆö dost thou laugh?" "So it is surely."
SKARPHEDINN: "Then here is a keepsake for thee" ŌĆö flicks Thrain's jaw-tooth; the laugher reels
back clutching his eye (impact off-frame). Skarphedinn's grin in the glow. Last: his voice
singing faintly inside the fire. FLOSI, turning away: "We shall have to boast of something
else than that Njal has been burnt in his house ŌĆö for there is no glory in that."

**c8_bright_body** (12s) ŌĆö Grey dawn, smoking ruin, silent crowd. The ox-hide is lifted:
Njal, Bergthora and the boy unburnt beneath, Njal's face luminous. ELDER: "Njal's body and
visage seem to me so bright that I have never seen any dead man's body so bright as this."
Nearby, a shape against the gable: Skarphedinn upright, axe sunk to mid-blade in the wall,
crosses branded on chest ŌĆö stylized silhouette in ash. M4 resolves to silence.

**c9_kari_vow** (8s) ŌĆö Kari on a hillside above, burnt-ragged, watching the smoke.
KARI (low): "Though all others take an atonement in their quarrels, yet will I take no
atonement. My son is still unavenged ŌĆö and I mean to take that on myself alone."

## EPILOGUE ŌĆö VENGEANCE AND PEACE (6 shots)

**d1_thing_battle** (12s) ŌĆö The Althing: the lawsuit collapsing in uproar ŌĆö then THORHALL
drives Skarphedinn's spear through his own swollen leg, strides out unhalting, and the
Thingfield explodes into battle. Kari catches a spear in flight and returns it. Brief, furious.
M5 (battle cue).

**d2_hall_peace** (10s) ŌĆö Hill of Laws, morning after; a dead young man on a bier. HALL OF
THE SIDE: "I will put no price on my son ŌĆö and yet will come forward, and grant both pledges
and peace to those who are my adversaries." The award: every Burner to fare abroad. Flosi
bows his head.

**d3_yule_feast** (13s) ŌĆö Orkney, Earl Sigurd's hall, Yule. GUNNAR LAMBI'S SON (eyepatch),
performing for the king: "How did Skarphedinn bear the Burning?" ŌĆö "Well at first, for a long
time; but the end of it was that he wept." KARI steps from the shadows by the door, sword
already swinging ŌĆö CUT to the king's board: cups toppling, men on their feet (kill off-frame).
KARI: "Many would say, Lord, that I have done this deed on your behalf ŌĆö to avenge your
henchman." FLOSI, to the stunned Earl: "Kari hath not done this without a cause: he is in no
atonement with us, and he only did what he had a right to do."

**d4_pilgrims** (8s, mute + M6) ŌĆö Montage: Flosi walking south, small under great mountains;
Rome, a blessing hand; Kari on the same road a season later. Both men grey-templed now.

**d5_reconciliation** (14s) ŌĆö Winter storm, Ingolf's Head. Kari, shipwrecked, walks through
snow to SWINEFELL and stands in the doorway, hollow-eyed. Flosi looks up ŌĆö springs from the
high-seat, KISSES him, and sets him in the seat beside his own. FLOSI: "There are few men like
Kari ŌĆö and I would that my mind were shapen altogether like his." At the hearth beyond,
Hildigunna watches; Kari meets her eyes. (Their marriage carried in one look + closing text.)

**d6_coda** (12s) ŌĆö Grey harbor. Old Flosi boards a leaning, weathered ship. VOICE (offscreen
sailor): "That ship is not seaworthy." FLOSI: "'Tis quite good enough for an old and
death-doomed man." The sail goes small on an iron sea. On-screen text (baked in start frame):
*"And of that ship no tidings were ever heard."* Beat. *"AND HERE WE END THE STORY OF BURNT
NJAL."* M6 (sparse warm) to silence.
