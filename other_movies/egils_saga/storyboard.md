# EGIL — a film of Egil's Saga

Source: W.C. Green translation (public domain), full text in `egils_saga_full.txt`
(92 chapters, 72k words; reader notes digested from 7 parallel passes).
Target: ~38 shots, ~5.5 min first cut, 480p fast draft per MOVIE_LESSONS workflow.

## Logline

The ugliest man in Iceland is its greatest poet. Across three generations a
family of shape-strong night-wolves feuds with the kings of Norway — and Egil
Skallagrimsson, who kills his first man at seven, buys back his own head from
his worst enemy with a poem, and is finally saved from grief not by vengeance
but by verse.

## Structure (prologue + 5 acts)

- **PROLOGUE — The Wolf's Prophecy** (Norway): Kveldulf foretells ruin from
  King Harald; golden Thorolf serves the king, is slandered, and burns at
  Sandness. The coffin chooses Borg.
- **ACT 1 — The Ugly Son** (Iceland): Egil the monstrous child-poet;
  Skallagrim's shape-strength at the sunset ball-game.
- **ACT 2 — The King's Enemy** (Norway): the poisoned horn at Bard's feast;
  Egil vs. Eirik Bloodaxe and Queen Gunnhild.
- **ACT 3 — Vinheath** (England): brother Thorolf falls in Athelstan's war;
  the wordless ring-over-the-fire scene.
- **ACT 4 — The Curse and the Head-Ransom**: the nithing pole; shipwrecked
  into Eirik's own court at York; twenty stanzas before dawn or his head.
- **ACT 5 — Sonatorrek and the Silver** (Iceland): Bodvar drowns; Thorgerd's
  milk trick; the lament that saves him; blind old Egil hides Athelstan's
  silver forever. Epilogue: the unbreakable skull.

Thematic spine: **Odin took his sons but gave him poetry — "boot for bale."**
Poetry saves his head at York and his life after Bodvar. The two family types
(fair doomed Thorolfs / dark surviving wolf-line) are the visual signature.

### PROLOGUE — THE WOLF'S PROPHECY

- **p1 (8s, silent)** — Dusk at Kveldulf's hall. The huge grey master stands
  among his household as the sun dies; his face empties; men edge away from
  him; he stares into the dark, wolf-still, firelight behind him.
  NARR: "They called him Kveldulf — the Evening-Wolf. Each day as the sun
  went down he grew sullen, and no man could speak with him. It was said he
  was shape-strong."
- **p2 (9s)** — Hall, day. Two of the king's messengers before Kveldulf's
  seat. KVELDULF: "Say this to your king: Kveldulf will sit at home. I think
  he has a whole load of good fortune, where our king has not a handful."
- **p3 (13s)** — Firelit hall, night. Kveldulf and golden Thorolf face to
  face. THOROLF K: "I am resolved to seek the king, and become his man."
  KVELDULF: "My foreboding is that we shall reap ruin from that king. Beware
  — keep within bounds, nor rival thy betters." They embrace on the shore;
  the sail dwindles.
- **p4 (7s, silent)** — The granary feast: shields hung all round the hall,
  Thorolf's five hundred men packing the benches; King Harald in the high
  seat scanning them, his face darkening to red; he says nothing.
  NARR: "Thorolf feasted the king with five hundred men — where the king
  brought three hundred. Kings do not forgive arithmetic."
- **p5 (10s)** — Candlelit corner, the small shrewd Harek close at the
  king's ear. HAREK: "He keeps a guard round him like a king. Keep Thorolf
  near thee, lord — that he make not himself too great for thee." Harald
  listens, quiet, believing.
- **p6 (12s)** — Night. Sandness hall ringed with torches; flames climb the
  birch-bark roof; the timber wall bursts OUTWARD and Thorolf comes through
  the gap, cutting toward the royal standard. THOROLF K: "Now am I but three
  feet short of my aim!" He falls forward at the king's feet. Harald looks
  down at him, unreadable. (Stage the death-stroke off-frame; see notes.)
- **p7 (8s)** — Kveldulf, aged overnight, in his seat. KVELDULF: "It is an
  old saw: he will be avenged who falls forward. But vengeance will not be
  mine. My sons must sail. There is new land found — westward. Iceland."
- **p8 (9s)** — Open grey ocean. A plain wooden coffin slides over the ship's
  rail into the wake; Skallagrim watches it go. KVELDULF (V.O., failing):
  "Make me a coffin, and put me overboard. It will go far otherwise than I
  think, if I do not come to Iceland and take land there."
- **p9 (8s, silent)** — An Icelandic creek at dawn: the coffin cast up on
  black sand. Skallagrim stands over it, then looks up at the rocky hill
  above the bay. NARR: "The dead man chose the land. They built at Borg —
  and there the story truly begins."

### ACT 1 — THE UGLY SON

- **a1 (8s, silent)** — Night moor, Iceland. A draught-horse walks the
  trackless heath — riding it, a tiny black-haired child, huge for three,
  grim as a stone. NARR: "Skallagrim had two sons. Thorolf, fair and beloved,
  like his uncle come again. And Egil: black-haired, ugly, and already too
  big. At three he was strong as a boy of seven — and he made verses."
- **a2 (12s)** — Yngvar's firelit feast. The three-year-old stands on the
  bench and declaims, word-perfect. EGIL CHILD: "Hasting I came to the
  hearth-fire of Yngvar, him who on heroes bestoweth gold. Thou wilt not find
  a doughtier song-smith of three winters." Old Yngvar laughs, delighted;
  down the table Skallagrim does not smile.
- **a3 (9s)** — Borg hall, day. Bera braids by the fire; seven-year-old Egil,
  knuckles skinned, chin bloody, stares ahead. BERA: "You have the makings of
  a freebooter, my son." EGIL CHILD: "Thus counselled my mother: for me shall
  they purchase a galley and good oars. So may I, high-standing, hew down
  many foemen." (His first killing stays off-screen; see notes.)
- **a4 (13s)** — Winter ball-game field at sunset. Skallagrim, losing to the
  boys, changes as the light dies — he swells, moves wrong, dashes a young
  man to the ground and turns on his own son. BRAK (running between):
  "Dost thou turn thy shape-strength, Skallagrim, against thy son?" He
  turns on her instead; she flees toward the headland. (Her death by the
  thrown stone: distant silhouette, off-frame impact; see notes.)
- **a5 (8s, silent)** — Borg hall, supper. Egil (12) rises, walks the length
  of the silent hall past his father's seat, and sits again. The man who kept
  Skallagrim's accounts is no longer at the table. Father and son stare at
  each other down the fire. NARR: "That evening Egil killed the man his
  father loved best. They did not speak all winter. In that family, grief
  was paid in kind."

### ACT 2 — THE KING'S ENEMY

- **b1 (8s, silent)** — Atla-isle banquet, torchlit. Horns crossing the
  boards; young-adult Egil drinking horn after horn; behind a pillar Bard
  and Queen Gunnhild bend over one horn together, her hand sifting something
  into the ale. NARR: "Grown, Egil went east — and made an enemy of a queen.
  Gunnhild: beautiful, shrewd, and of magic cunning."
- **b2 (10s)** — Close: Egil pricks his palm with his knife, carves runes on
  the horn, smears them dark. EGIL: "Write we runes around the horn, redden
  all the spell with blood. Learn that health abides in ale — holy ale that
  Bard hath blessed." The horn BURSTS asunder; ale hisses in the straw.
- **b3 (8s)** — Doorway, near dark. Bard offers a farewell cup; Egil drains
  it, and his sword flashes once; Egil is gone into the pitch-black night as
  torches flare behind him. NARR: "Bard fell dead in his own doorway. And the
  king's hunt began."
- **b4 (8s, silent)** — Dawn sound. Egil swims a wide cold strait, weapons
  bundled in his cloak upon his back; behind him, an island; ahead, nothing
  but grey water.
- **b5 (10s)** — Eirik's hall. GUNNHILD: "Thou lendest easy ear to talk. Egil
  has slain thy friends and thy steward — I reckon no odds between him and
  his brother." EIRIK: "Egil shall not be long harboured in my realm."

### ACT 3 — VINHEATH

- **c1 (8s, silent)** — Vinheath: a wall of tall tents between wood and
  river; hazel-poles mark the field; skeleton crews swarm to look numerous.
  NARR: "The brothers took service with Athelstan of England, against Olaf
  king of Scots. Athelstan bought a week with empty tents and false envoys.
  Then the armies met at Vinheath."
- **c2 (10s)** — Pre-battle camp, dawn. EGIL: "I will not that I and Thorolf
  be parted in the battle." THOROLF S: "Brother, you will have your way —
  but it is the king's array." EGIL (low): "This separation I shall often
  rue."
- **c3 (10s)** — The wood-edge. Thorolf presses forward along the trees,
  shield slung, halberd two-handed — and armed men burst from the very wood
  he trusted; many spears strike him at once; he falls; his standard reels
  back. (Blood minimal; impacts masked by shields; see notes.)
- **c4 (10s)** — Egil, across the field, hears the shout, sees the standard
  retreat — and RUNS between the armies, sword out, roaring; he cuts down the
  ambusher-earl in three strokes; the line turns behind him. NARR: "Egil took
  Earl Adils' life, and the day turned. Athelstan won England's greatest
  victory. It cost Egil his brother."
- **c5 (8s)** — The mound. Egil clasps a gold bracelet on each of his dead
  brother's wrists. EGIL: "Green grows on soil of Vinheath, grass o'er my
  noble brother. But we our woe — a sorrow worse than death-pang — must
  bear."
- **c6 (14s, silent)** — THE RING OVER THE FIRE. Athelstan's hall: the long
  fire down the middle; Egil fully armed in the facing high seat, helmet on,
  shield at his feet, sword half-drawn then clashed back; one eyebrow sunk to
  the cheek, the other cocked to the hair-roots; he will not drink. Athelstan
  studies him — then draws his own sword, hangs a great gold arm-ring on the
  point, rises, and reaches it across the flames. Egil stands, extends his
  sword, hooks the ring off point-to-point, slides it on his arm. His brows
  settle level. He drinks. (Signature shot — all faces, fire, and steel.)

### ACT 4 — THE CURSE AND THE HEAD-RANSOM

- **d1 (9s)** — The Gula-thing: judges within the hazel-rope ring; armed men
  wade in and CUT the sacred ropes at the queen's nod; the court scatters.
  EGIL (shouted over the chaos): "Then I ban these lands! I denounce him who
  holds them: law-breaker, peace-breaker, and ACCURSED!" NARR (bridge):
  "An inheritance of his wife's, stolen with the crown's blessing. Egil
  answered in the old way."
- **d2 (12s)** — Dusk headland. Egil raises a hazel pole with a horse's
  skull set upon it, turning the skull to face the mainland. EGIL: "Here set
  I up a curse-pole, and this curse I turn on King Eirik and Queen Gunnhild —
  and on the land-spirits of this land: may they all wander astray, and never
  find their home, till they have driven King Eirik and Gunnhild from the
  land." He cuts runes into the pole. Wind rising.
- **d3 (8s, silent)** — Storm. Egil's ship driven in white surf onto an
  English shore and broken. Egil wades out of the sea. NARR: "The curse
  worked. Eirik was driven from Norway — to England, where Athelstan gave
  him York to hold. And Gunnhild worked a spell of her own: that Egil should
  find no rest till she had seen him. His ship broke at Humber-mouth — in
  Eirik Bloodaxe's new kingdom."
- **d4 (8s)** — York, night. A hooded giant at a torchlit door; Arinbjorn's
  face when he opens it. ARINBJORN: "Egil. There is nothing for it now. You
  shall bring the king your head — and I will be your spokesman."
- **d5 (11s)** — Eirik's hall, torches, drawn steel. Egil kneels, clasps the
  king's foot. GUNNHILD: "Why is Egil not slain at once? Have you forgotten,
  king, what he has done — your friends, your kin, your own son?" ARINBJORN:
  "Night-slaying is murder, king. Give him till morning. If he has spoken
  evil of thee, he can atone in words of praise that shall live for all
  time."
- **d6 (10s)** — The upper room, night. Egil at the one window, murmuring
  verse — and a swallow at the sill twitters, twitters, breaking every line.
  On the roof above, Arinbjorn keeps watch through the dark; something not
  quite a bird flees the eaves before dawn. (Gunnhild's shape — imply only.)
- **d7 (13s)** — Morning court, packed and hostile; Eirik bolt upright,
  staring; Egil stands alone and his voice fills the hall. EGIL: "Westward I
  sailed the wave; within me Odin gave the sea of song I bear. My mind a
  galleon fraught with load of minstrel thought... Glory and fame gat Eirik's
  name." The court is silent. (Twenty stanzas; we hear first and refrain.)
- **d8 (11s)** — EIRIK (slowly, staring): "The poem is well delivered. I give
  thee thy head this time — because thou camest freely into my power. But
  know this for sure: it is no reconciliation." Egil bows once, ugly and
  unbroken. NARR (bridge): "So Egil bought his head with a poem. The
  Head-Ransom, men call it, to this day."

### ACT 5 — SONATORREK AND THE SILVER

- **e1 (7s, silent)** — Iceland, years on. A squall on White-river firth at
  dusk: an eight-oared boat, heavily loaded, vanishes among the whitecaps.
  One empty oar washes ashore. NARR: "Egil went home to Borg, and grew old,
  and had sons. Bodvar was the best of them — fair, like the Thorolfs. He
  was seventeen when the boat went down."
- **e2 (10s, silent)** — Egil, grey now, rides the shore with his son's body
  across his saddle-bow; at the mound on Digra-ness he lifts the boy — and
  his kirtle and hose SPLIT off his swollen arms and calves as he strains.
  He lays Bodvar beside Skallagrim's bones and stands in the closing dark.
- **e3 (7s, silent)** — Borg hall. The bed-closet door shuts; the bolt drops.
  Asgerd stands before the locked panel, then turns to a rider: "Fetch
  Thorgerd." NARR: "He locked himself in his bed-closet, and would take
  neither food nor drink. He meant to die. Three days. Then his daughter
  came."
- **e4 (14s)** — The closet door. THORGERD (through the wood): "Father, open
  the door. I will that we both travel the same road." The bolt slides. In
  the dark, side by side. She chews; he stirs. EGIL: "What is it, daughter —
  you are chewing something?" THORGERD: "Samphire. It shortens life. And
  here — water." He drains the horn — stops. EGIL: "We are deceived. This is
  milk." He bites a sherd clean out of the horn and hurls it down.
- **e5 (13s)** — THORGERD: "Then let us lengthen our lives, father — long
  enough that you make a funeral poem on Bodvar. I will carve it on a roller.
  Then we can die — if we like." A long silence. EGIL (a ruin of a voice):
  "Much doth it task me my tongue to move... Me Ran, the sea-queen, roughly
  hath shaken: I stand of beloved ones stript and all bare." His voice
  gathers strength as the fire rises.
- **e6 (10s)** — Egil, risen, at the high seat, the household gathered; the
  lament ends. EGIL: "The god broke faith and friendship, false in my need —
  yet he gave me poesy faultless, boot for bale. And on Digra-ness Hel waits.
  Dauntless in bearing, her death-blow I bide." NARR (bridge): "The
  Sonatorrek — the Loss of Sons. The poem brought him back. Odin took his
  sons, and paid him in verse."
- **e7 (9s)** — Moss-fell, years later. Blind white Egil gropes along the
  fire; serving-women laugh; the cook shoos him from the hearth. EGIL (a dry
  ember of a voice): "Blind near the blaze I wander, and beg the fire-maid's
  pardon. Yet England's mighty monarch me whilom greatly honoured — and
  princes once with pleasure the poet's accents heard."
- **e8 (10s)** — Egil and Thordis by the fire, conspirators. EGIL: "I mean to
  carry Athelstan's silver to the Hill of Laws — and sow it broadcast into
  the biggest crowd of the Thing. Kicks there will be, I fancy, and blows.
  Nay — it may end in a general fight of all the assembled Thing." THORDIS
  (delighted): "A famous plan! It will be remembered as long as Iceland is
  inhabited."
- **e9 (10s, silent)** — Night: the blind old man rides out with two thralls
  and two iron-bound chests. Morning: Egil alone in the holt, leading his
  horse, chests gone, thralls gone; he says nothing, ever. NARR: "They
  forbade him the Thing, so one night the silver went into the earth instead
  — and the two thralls with it. Blind, in his ninth decade, he killed his
  last two men. English pennies still wash out of the gill after thaws. The
  hoard has never been found."
- **e10 (10s, silent)** — Epilogue. A churchyard, generations later, men in
  early-Christian dress; a priest holds up an unearthed skull — massive,
  wave-ridged like a shell. He strikes it full force with the back of a
  hand-axe: it whitens. It does not crack. He turns it in the light.
  NARR: "When they moved the bones, the skull would not break. Heavy and
  wave-marked, like a shell. It was a hard head to take, alive or dead.
  And so ends this story." TITLE (baked into the frame): "AND SO ENDS THIS
  STORY."
