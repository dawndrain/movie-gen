# THE VARIANCE — storyboard (first pass, tv1)

Source: `the_variance.md`. ~24 shots, ~3.3 min. Quiet dystopian sci-fi
(THX-1138 / Never Let Me Go / Equals). Sincerity concentrated, no comedy.

## Visual grammar
- **Baseline world** (acts 1, 2, 5): desaturated teal-grey grade, soft overcast
  light, locked-off static camera, symmetrical compositions, people as fluid.
- **The eleven days** (act 3): warm golden light, saturated living color,
  gentle handheld drift. The grade IS the story.
- **The collapse** (act 4): the warmth curdles — overbright, harsh, trembling.
- Act 5 mirrors act 1 shot-for-shot, drained again.

## Sound design
- Sparse third-person **narrator** (verbatim story lines) mixed at ASSEMBLY over
  silent/ambient shots — never generated in Seedance (avoids spontaneous
  narration; fully removable if it tramples).
- Dialogue: story-verbatim for Petra + bench scenes; two invented Dez lines.
- Music: 5 Sonilo cues (baseline hum, drawer/wonder, alive, collapse, elegy),
  mixed 0.07–0.15, silence under the bench scene until "I was alive."

## Cast
- **OREN** — mid-20s, placid gentle face, slate-blue worker jumpsuit (24-F).
- **PETRA** — late 40s, grey-streaked pinned hair, charcoal supervisor uniform (22-G).
- **DEZ** — late 20s, loose curls, rust-orange scarf + patched olive jacket (S-class);
  the only warm color in the city.

### Act 4 — Variance collapse
| t18 | 8 | Grey dawn, the courtyard bench. Oren wakes; the light HURTS. Hands shaking. The drawer is a wound. | NARR: "The crash came on the twelfth day. An F-class brain was not built for this. Blow the channel open, and there is no infrastructure to manage the flow." |
| t19 | 11 | Petra finds him on the bench. She is afraid — for him. OREN (too fast): "I can't stop it. There's too much. I opened something and I can't close it — and I can't hold it open either." PETRA: "I know." She sits. She doesn't touch him. | dialogue |
| t20 | 13 | The core. OREN (crying, first tears of his life): "I was alive. For eleven days — I was alive." PETRA: "Yes." OREN: "Does it have to stop?" She doesn't answer. She looks at the dead stem in the pavement. | dialogue |
| t21 | 8 | Recalibration suite: white, kind, clinical. Oren lies back calmly under a soft instrument halo. No restraints. Nobody is cruel. | NARR: "They recalibrated him. It wasn't punishment — G's didn't punish. It was restoration, they called it. A return to specification." |

### Act 5 — Baseline

| t23 | 7 | The fabrication hall. Oren at his station. Steady. Metrics at baseline. | ambient |
| t24 | 10 | Close: his hand presses flat against the cool surface of the workstation. He looks at it. The lines. The warmth. Hold. Slow fade. | NARR: "He didn't know why he did this. But he didn't stop." |
