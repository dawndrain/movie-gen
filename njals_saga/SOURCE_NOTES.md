Line numbers refer to `njal_dasent.txt`. Front matter ends at line 1509; saga text ends

## The shape of the saga (for structure)

1. **Prologue generation (ch. I–VIII)**: Hrut, Gunnhilda's curse, the dower feud.
   Key beat: child Hallgerda and the "thief's eyes" prophecy — the saga's inciting omen.
2. **Hallgerda's husbands (IX–XVII)**: Thorwald and Glum both die by her foster-father
   Thiostolf's axe. Pattern established: she is bad luck made flesh. She LAUGHS at Glum's death.
3. **Gunnar rises (XIX–XXXIII)**: hero intro, wins Unna's dower back by the Huckster Hedinn
   con (Njal scripting the future), wins the singing bill abroad, marries Hallgerda over
   everyone's warnings.
4. **The wives' feud (XXXV–XLV)**: Bergthora vs Hallgerda, alternating killings, Gunnar and
   Njal passing the same purse of silver back and forth. The SLAP (XLVIII).
5. **Gunnar's doom (XLVII–LXXVII)**: Otkell, the ford fight, Njal's two-condition prophecy,
   the horse-fight, Knafahills, outlawry, "Fair is the Lithe," the siege, the bowstring
   refusal, death, the singing cairn.
6. **Thrain arc (LXXXVII–XCII)**: Killing-Hrapp sheltered, quarrel, Skarphedinn's ice-slide
   killing of Thrain (jaw-teeth on the ice — the tooth returns at the Burning).
7. **Hoskuld arc (XCIII–CXI)**: Njal fosters Thrain's son Hoskuld (gold-ring scene), the
   Conversion of Iceland (year 1000, Thorgeir under the cloak), Mord's slander campaign,
   the murder of Hoskuld in his cornfield ("God help me, and forgive you!"), Hildigunna
   locks the bloody cloak in her chest.
8. **The road to the fire (CXIV–CXXVI)**: bloody-cloak whetting of Flosi, the begging tour
   of the booths (four chiefs describe Skarphedinn), the broken settlement (scarf/breeks
   insults), the oath at the Great Rift, portents (Wolf's-ride, last supper, gore vision).
9. **THE BURNING (CXXVII–CXXXII)**: the fatal retreat indoors, women let out, Njal and
   Bergthora under the ox-hide with grandson Thord, Kari's flaming escape, Skarphedinn's
   death and fire-song, the bright corpse.
10. **Law and battle (CXXXIII–CXLIV)**: the great Althing lawsuit, Eyjolf's venue trap,
    Mord's blunder, Thorhall lancing his boil with Skarphedinn's spear, the Battle at the
    Althing, Hall of the Side's "no price on my son," exile of the Burners.
11. **Kari's vengeance (CXLV–CLVII)**: ambushes with comic braggart Bjorn, the Yule-feast
    beheading in Orkney, Clontarf (Valkyries' Woof of War, Brodir, King Brian), Kol's head
    counting "ten."
12. **Reconciliation (CLVIII)**: both pilgrimage to Rome; Kari shipwrecks at Flosi's door;
    Flosi kisses him, seats him in the high-seat, gives him Hildigunna in marriage.
    Coda: old Flosi sails in a death-doomed ship, never heard of again.
    "And here we end the STORY of BURNT NJAL."

## CHARACTER BIBLE (verbatim descriptions → anchor prompts)

**NJAL** — "wealthy in goods, and handsome of face; **no beard grew on his chin**. He was so
great a lawyer, that his match was not to be found. Wise too he was, and foreknowing and
foresighted... Gentle and generous." Dress at the Thing: "clad in a blue cape, and had a felt
hat on his head, and a small axe in his hand." Corpse: "so bright that I have never seen any
dead man's body so bright as this." THE BEARDLESSNESS IS PLOT-CRITICAL (all the insults).

**GUNNAR of Lithend** — "tall... best skilled in arms of all men... he smote so swiftly with
his sword, that three seemed to flash through the air at once... could leap more than his own
height, with all his war-gear... handsome of feature, and fair skinned. His nose was straight,
and a little turned up at the end. He was blue-eyed and bright-eyed, and ruddy-cheeked. His
hair thick, and of good hue, and hanging down in comely curls." Weapon: the bill (halberd)
that SINGS before killings. Often in a red kirtle.

**HALLGERDA** — "the fairest of women... tall of stature... called 'Longcoat'. She was
fair-haired, and had so much of it that she could hide herself in it; but she was lavish and
hard-hearted." As child: "hair soft as silk... came down to her waist." Costume: "a red
kirtle, and had thrown over her a scarlet cloak trimmed with needlework down to the waist."
Dasent: "that noble frame, so fair and tall, and yet with so foul a heart."

**BERGTHORA** — "a very high-spirited, brave-hearted woman, but somewhat hard-tempered."
Dies by choice: "I was given away to Njal young, and I have promised him this, that we would
both share the same fate."

**SKARPHEDINN** — "tall... his hair was dark brown, with crisp curly locks; he had good eyes;
his features were sharp, and his face ashen pale, his nose turned up and his front teeth stuck
out, and his mouth was very ugly. Still he was the most soldier-like of men." Thing costume:
"a blue kirtle and gray breeks, and black shoes... a silver belt about him, and that same axe
in his hand with which he slew Thrain, and which he called the 'ogress of war,' a round
buckler, and a silken band round his brow, and his hair brushed back behind his ears."
Tell: grins/smiles when speaking of death; "the sweat burst out upon his brow, and red flecks
came over his cheeks." Four chiefs call him "pale-faced... unlucky-looking... troll-like."

**KARI Solmund's son** — entrance: "stood a man by the mast, who was clad in a silken kirtle,
and had a gilded helm, and his hair was both fair and thick; that man had a spear inlaid with
gold in his hand." Sword "Life-luller." "He never abused his foes, and never threatened them."
Flosi: "that man hath now got away who comes next to Gunnar of Lithend in all things."

**FLOSI** — "dwelt at Swinefell, and was a mighty chief. He was tall of stature, and strong
withal, the most forward and boldest of men." Emotional tell: "sometimes he was bloodred in
the face, and sometimes ashy pale as withered grass, and sometimes blue as death." Later:
"the merriest of men, and the best of hosts... he had most of the chieftain in him of all
the men of his time."

**HILDIGUNNA** — "a proud, high-spirited maiden, and one of the fairest of women... the
grimmest and hardest-hearted of all women; but still a woman of open hand and heart when any
fitting call was made upon her." "Hildigunna laughed a cold laugh."

**MORD Valgard's son** — "he was crafty and spiteful... very envious of Gunnar of Lithend.
He was wealthy, so far as goods went, but had not many friends." Trait: everyone who talks
to him "thought as though Mord would be true to him." The saga's Iago.

**HOSKULD the Whiteness Priest** (Thrain's son, Njal's foster-son) — "both tall and strong;
the fairest of men to look on, and well-haired; blithe of speech, bountiful, well-behaved...
fairspoken to all men, and much beloved." Dies in Flosi's gift: a scarlet embroidered cloak.

**HRUT** (prologue) — "handsome, tall and strong... mild of temper; one of the wisest of
men — stern towards his foes, but a good counsellor on great matters."

**RANNVEIG** (Gunnar's mother) — grim, prophetic, keeps the bill after Gunnar dies: "he alone
should have it as his own, who was ready to avenge Gunnar."

**GIZUR THE WHITE** — honorable enemy leader; twice refuses arson: "That shall never be,
though I knew that my life lay on it."

**HALL OF THE SIDE** — the saga's peacemaker. "He rode in a blue cape, and had a little axe
studded with silver in his hand." Waives his dead son's fine to buy peace.

**BJORN THE WHITE** (comedy relief, Kari arc) — "always boasting and praising himself, but
his housewife thought that bad. He was sharpsighted and swift of foot."

**THORHALL Asgrim's son** — Njal's law-pupil; leg boil "as big and swollen as a woman's
thigh"; hears of the case collapsing, drives Skarphedinn's spear through his own boil and
strides out unhalting to start the Battle at the Althing.

## MASTER DIALOGUE BANK (verbatim, by arc)

### Prologue
- Hauskuld: "Come hither to me, daughter." … "What dost thou think of this maiden? Is she not fair?"
- HRUT: "Fair enough is this maid, and many will smart for it, but this I know not, whence
  thief's eyes have come into our race."
- Gunnhilda's curse: "I lay this spell on thee that thou mayest never have any pleasure in
  living with that woman on whom thy heart is set in Iceland."

### Hallgerda's husbands
- Hallgerda: "Thine axe is bloody; what hast thou done?" / Thiostolf: "I have done now what
  will cause thee to be wedded a second time."
- Hrut's wedding prophecy: "The day will come when thou wilt have to waste thy goods for
  Hallgerda's sake, so hold thy hand now."

### Gunnar & Hallgerda
- Gunnar: "How wouldst thou answer were I to ask for thee?" / "That can not be in thy mind."
  / "It is though."
- Hrut to Gunnar: "she is much mixed up with ill report, and I will not cheat thee in anything."
- Njal: "Because from her will arise all kind of ill if she comes hither east." / Gunnar:
  "Never shall she spoil our friendship." / Njal: "thou wilt have always to make atonement for her."
- Flyting: Hallgerda: "Thou hast hangnails on every finger, and Njal is beardless." /
  Bergthora: "but Thorwald, thy husband, was not beardless, and yet thou plottedst his death."
- Gunnar: "Home I will go, and it were more seemly... never will I be egged on by thee like a fool."
- Hallgerda's exit: "Mind this, Bergthora, that we shall meet again."
- Skarphedinn (smiling): "Thralls are men of more mettle than of yore... but now they will do
  naught but kill." / Njal: "More men now become man-slayers than I weened."
- Bergthora: "even as much money shall be paid for Kol as time goes on."

### The slap & Gunnar's doom
- Gunnar: "Ill indeed is it if I am a partaker with thieves." (slap) — "She said she would
  bear that slap in mind and repay it if she could."
- Gunnar: "I would like to know whether I am by so much the less brisk and bold than other
  men, because I think more of killing men than they?"
- NJAL'S TWO CONDITIONS: "never slay more than one man in the same stock, and never break the
  peace which good men and true make between thee and others... if that should come to pass
  of which I have warned thee, then thou wilt have but a little while to live; but otherwise,
  thou wilt come to be an old man."
- Gunnar: "Dost thou know what will be thine own death?" / Njal: "I know it." / "What?" /
  "That which all would be the last to think."
- Gunnar: "Death will come to me when it will come, wherever I may be, if that is my fate."
- THE TURN-BACK: "Fair is the Lithe; so fair that it has never seemed to me so fair; the corn
  fields are white to harvest, and the home mead is mown; and now I will ride back home, and
  not fare abroad at all."
- Kolskegg: "I will never do a base thing in this... tell this to my kinsmen and to my mother,
  that I never mean to see Iceland again, for I shall soon learn that thou art dead, brother."
- Gunnar waking: "Thou hast been sorely treated, Sam, my fosterling, and this warning is so
  meant that our two deaths will not be far apart."
- Thorgrim the Easterling, dying: "Find that out for yourselves; but this I am sure of, that
  his bill is at home."
- THE BOWSTRING: Gunnar: "Give me two locks of thy hair, and ye two, my mother and thou,
  twist them together into a bowstring for me." / Hallgerda: "Does aught lie on it?" /
  "My life lies on it; for they will never come to close quarters with me if I can keep them
  off with my bow." / "Well! now I will call to thy mind that slap on the face which thou
  gavest me; and I care never a whit whether thou holdest out a long while or a short." /
  Gunnar: "Every one has something to boast of, and I will ask thee no more for this." /
  Rannveig: "Thou behavest ill, and this shame shall long be had in mind."
- Gizur's eulogy: "We have now laid low to earth a mighty chief, and hard work has it been,
  and the fame of this defence of his shall last as long as men live in this land."
- Mord: "Let us burn him house and all." / Gizur: "That shall never be, though I knew that my
  life lay on it." (FORESHADOWING)
- Ghost-song refrain: "I will die the prop of battle, / Sooner die than yield an inch."

### Thrain & Hoskuld
- Skarphedinn: "I am tying my shoe." (then the twelve-ell leap, the ice-slide, Thrain's
  jaw-teeth on the ice) / Kari: "This was done like a man."
- Njal to boy Hoskuld: "Knowest thou what brought thy father to his death?" / Boy: "I know
  that Skarphedinn slew him; but we need not keep that in mind, when an atonement has been
  made for it, and a full price paid for him." / Njal: "Better answered than asked... and
  thou wilt live to be a good man and true."
- Thorgeir the Lawspeaker: "if there be a sundering of the laws, then there will be a
  sundering of the peace... This is the beginning of our laws, that all men shall be
  Christian here in the land."
- Valgard's deathbed: "set them by the ears by tale-bearing, so that Njal's sons may slay
  Hauskuld; but there are many who will have the blood-feud after him, and so Njal's sons
  will be slain in that quarrel."
- Hoskuld on the slander: "though there were aught in it... then would I far rather suffer
  death at their hands than work them any harm."
- Skarphedinn: "Don't try to turn on thy heel, Whiteness priest."
- HOSKULD DYING: "God help me, and forgive you!"
- Njal: "methinks it were better to have lost two of my sons, and that Hauskuld lived...
  the sweetest light of my eyes was quenched." / Skarphedinn: "What will come after?" /
  Njal: "MY DEATH, AND THE DEATH OF MY WIFE AND OF ALL MY SONS." / Kari: "What dost thou
  foretell for me?" / "They will have hard work to go against thy good fortune."
- Hildigunna over the body: "It were a manly deed, if one man had been at it."

### The bloody cloak & broken peace
- Hildigunna: "What vengeance or help shall I have of thee?" — (throws the cloak, "the gore
  rattled down all over him") — "This cloak, Flosi, thou gavest to Hauskuld, and now I will
  give it back to thee; he was slain in it, and I call God and all good men to witness, that
  I adjure thee, by all the might of thy Christ, and by thy manhood and bravery, to take
  vengeance for all those wounds which he had on his dead body, or else to be called every
  man's dastard."
- Flosi: "Thou art the greatest hell-hag, and thou wishest that we should take that course
  which will be the worst for all of us. But 'women's counsel is ever cruel'."
- Flosi: "Ill seed has been sown, and so an ill crop will spring from it."
- Njal's plea: "I loved Hauskuld more than my own sons, and when I heard that he was slain,
  methought the sweetest light of my eyes was quenched."
- Flosi (scarf): "I think that thy father the 'Beardless Carle' must have given it, for many
  know not who look at him whether he is more a man than a woman." / Skarphedinn: "he has had
  sons by his wife, and few of our kinsfolk have fallen unatoned by our house, so that we
  have not had vengeance for them." (throws blue breeks) "Because thou art the sweetheart of
  the Swinefell's goblin, if, as men say, he does indeed turn thee into a woman every ninth night."
- Flosi: "Go we now home; one fate shall befall us all."
- The oath: "we shall ride to Bergthorsknoll with all our band, and fall on Njal's sons with
  fire and sword, and not turn away before they are all dead."

### Portents & the Burning
- Bergthora: "Now shall ye choose your meat to-night, so that each may have what he likes
  best; for this evening is the last that I shall set meat before my household."
- Njal's vision: "Methinks I see all round the room, and it seems as though the gable wall
  were thrown down, but the whole board and the meat on it is one gore of blood."
- Njal: "my will is that our men go indoors, for they had hard work to master Gunnar of
  Lithend, though he was alone to meet them."
- Skarphedinn: "these will fall on us at once with fire... Besides, I am unwilling to let
  myself be stifled indoors like a fox in his earth." … "still I may well humour my father in
  this, by being burnt indoors along with him, for I am not afraid of my death."
- Flosi: "Now are they all 'fey,' since they have gone indoors." … "now there are but two
  choices left, and neither of them good. One is to turn away, and that is our death; the
  other, to set fire to the house, and burn them inside it; and that is a deed which we shall
  have to answer for heavily before God, since we are Christian men ourselves; but still we
  must take to that counsel."
- Skarphedinn: "What, lads! are ye lighting a fire, or are ye taking to cooking?"
- Njal to the women: "Keep up your hearts, nor utter shrieks, for this is but a passing
  storm... put your faith in God, and believe that He is so merciful that He will not let us
  burn both in this world and the next."
- Flosi: "I will offer thee, master Njal, leave to go out, for it is unworthy that thou
  shouldst burn indoors." / NJAL: "I will not go out, for I am an old man, and little fitted
  to avenge my sons, but I will not live in shame."
- Bergthora: "I was given away to Njal young, and I have promised him this, that we would
  both share the same fate."
- Njal: "We will go to our bed, and lay us down; I have long been eager for rest."
- The boy Thord: "Thou hast promised me this, grandmother, that we should never part so long
  as I wished to be with thee; but methinks it is much better to die with thee and Njal than
  to live after you."
- Kari/Skarphedinn parting: "Leap thou out here, and I will help thee..." / "Thou shalt leap
  first; but I will leap straightway on thy heels." / Kari: "still this parting of ours will
  be in such wise that we shall never see one another more." / Skarphedinn: "It joys me,
  brother-in-law, to think that if thou gettest away thou wilt avenge me."
- Gunnar Lambi's son: "Weepest thou now, Skarphedinn?" / "Not so, but true it is that the
  smoke makes one's eyes smart, but is it as it seems to me, dost thou laugh?" / "So it is
  surely, and I have never laughed since thou slewest Thrain on Markfleet." / "He now is a
  keepsake for thee" — throws Thrain's jaw-tooth into his eye.
- Flosi: "Men will call it both a mighty deed and an ill deed, but that can't be helped now."
  … "We shall have to boast of something else than that Njal has been burnt in his house,
  for there is no glory in that."
- Finding the bones: "Njal's body and visage seem to me so bright that I have never seen any
  dead man's body so bright as this." Skarphedinn upright against the gable, legs burnt to
  the knees, axe driven to mid-blade in the wall, crosses branded on chest and back.

### Law, battle, vengeance
- Kari to Mord: "thou art both a coward and heartless... see that thou pleadest it without
  fear, for thy life lies on it."
- Snorri to Eyjolf (bribe ring): "may this ring not be thy death!"
- Skarphedinn vs Thorkel foulmouth: "sheathe thy sword and sit thee down, or I drive the axe

- Snorri: "I ween thou hast not long to live." / Skarphedinn: "That is well, for that is a

- Kari: "Though all others take an atonement in their quarrels, yet will I take no atonement

- Yule feast — Sigtrygg: "How did Skarphedinn bear the Burning?" / Gunnar Lambi's son (the

  (Kari's sword; the head "spun off on to the board before the king.")
- Kari: "Many would say, Lord, that I have done this deed on your behalf, to avenge your

  us, and he only did what he had a right to do."

- Kari's proverb: "Bare is back without brother behind it."
- Valkyries (Woof of War): "See! warp is stretched / For warriors' fall, / Lo! weft in loom /

  weird sisters, / Our warwinning woof."

### Reconciliation & coda
- Flosi: "There are few men like Kari, and I would that my mind were shapen altogether like his."
- (Kari shipwrecked, walks to Swinefell to "put Flosi's manhood to the proof"; Flosi springs
  up, kisses him, seats him beside himself in the high-seat. Kari marries Hildigunna.)
- Flosi on the old ship: quite good enough "for an old and death-doomed man"... "But of that
  ship no tidings were ever heard."
- Final line: "And here we end the STORY of BURNT NJAL."

## SETTINGS / LOCATION PLATES

- **Lithend** — Gunnar's farm on the Lithe slope: wooden hall "roofed with beams above, and
  there were window-slits under the beams"; sunken road between fences above the yard; corn
  fields "white to harvest"; the cairn.
- **Bergthorsknoll** — Njal's homestead on a knoll in flat lowland; strong house, loft/
  cross-trees, gable walls, vetch-stack just above the house (the kindling).
- **The Althing at Thingvellir** — Hill of Laws, rows of turf-and-canvas booths, Axewater
  river and bridge, the Great Rift (Almannagja) lava walls.
- **Markfleet, frozen** — "flowing between sheets of ice on both sides... a great sheet of
  ice... as smooth and slippery as glass" (the ice-slide killing).
- **Rangriver ford** — flat stones, mud, milking-post (Gunnar vs eight).
- **Ossaby** — Hoskuld's farm: fence + cornfield, "the weather was good, and the sun just risen."
- **Swinefell** — Flosi's chieftain hall in the east (bloody cloak; final reconciliation).
- **Orkney: Earl Sigurd's Yule hall**; **Clontarf/Dublin** battlefield; the North Sea.
- Iceland at large: black sand, lava fields, green home-meads, hay-work, turf halls.

## PRODUCTION / CONTENT WARNINGS (per MOVIE_LESSONS)

- **"slave" is a banned submission word** — the saga is full of thralls. Use "thrall,"
  "house-carle," "serving-man." Watch any modern paraphrase.
- **Child death in the fire** (grandson Thord under the ox-hide) — likely nsfw-trigger if
  staged literally. Stage: family lies down calmly, ox-hide drawn over, cut away to exterior
  fire. Never show a child in flames. (Walter's Deal lesson: change WHAT HAPPENS, not words.)
- **Beheadings** (Helgi at the burning, Yule feast, Kol) — off-screen or reaction-shot only.
- **The slap** — show wind-up and her hand going to her cheek, not the impact frame.
- **Gore-cloak** ("the gore rattled down") — use dried dark-brown staining, avoid the words
  "gore/blood" in image prompts if flagged; "the stiff dark-stained cloak."
- Skarphedinn's burnt corpse: keep stylized (silhouette in ash, axe in gable).
