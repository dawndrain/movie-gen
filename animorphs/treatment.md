# THE HORK-BAJIR CHRONICLES — film treatment (first pass)

Adapted from K.A. Applegate's *The Hork-Bajir Chronicles*. Frame story: Jara Hamee
tells Tobias (red-tailed hawk) the story of the fall of the Hork-Bajir homeworld,
a generation before the Animorphs' war.

Target: ~42 shots × ~8s ≈ 6 minutes. 480p std Seedance, Nano Banana anchors/frames.

## Cast (anchors)

- **dak** — Dak Hamee, young Hork-Bajir "seer": a genius born once in a generation
  to a gentle, simple species. Curious eyes, unscarred blades.
- **jagil** — Jagil Hullan, Dak's friend. Stockier, darker bark-brown skin. Later a
  Controller.
- **aldrea** — Aldrea-Iskillion-Falan, adolescent female Andalite, daughter of Seerow.
  Defiant, quick. Lavender-blue fur.
- **seerow** — Prince Seerow, her father. Demoted for "Seerow's Kindness" (gave the
  Yeerks technology). Graying fur, kind tired eyes.
- **alloran** — War-Prince Alloran-Semitur-Corrass. Hard, scarred, military harness.
  Author of the quantum virus.
- **esplin** — Esplin 9466 in his Hork-Bajir host: rigid, imperious, black-and-red
  Yeerk harness. The future Visser Three.
- **arn** — an Arn: the vain, brilliant, 4-foot iridescent bio-engineers who created
  the Hork-Bajir as gardeners and hid underground.
- **jara** — Jara Hamee (frame story), older battle-scarred Hork-Bajir on Earth.
- **toby** — Toby Hamee, Jara's daughter, a young seer. Named for Tobias.
- **tobias** — red-tailed hawk.

## Locations (plates)

- **loc_valley** — Hork-Bajir homeworld: trees two kilometers tall, teal canopy,
  Father Deep (indigo mist below), Mother Sky above.
- **loc_deep** — the lightless root-world at the base of the trees; bioluminescence;
  engineered monsters.
- **loc_arn_city** — the Arn's hidden cavern city: amber-and-jade towers, gene tanks.
- **loc_dome** — Andalite outpost dome on a high ridge.
- **loc_poolship** — Yeerk pool ship: steel cavern, leaden pool, infestation piers.
- **loc_earth_valley** — the free Hork-Bajir colony valley on Earth (frame story).

## Voice locks

- Hork-Bajir (Dak, Jara, Jagil, Toby): deep, gravelly, warm; simple halting speech.
  Dak's speech grows fluent as Aldrea teaches him.
- Aldrea / Seerow / Alloran (Andalites): calm telepathic voice; **Andalites have no
  mouths** — main eyes fix on the listener, no lips ever.
- Esplin: cold, precise, silky male voice (through Hork-Bajir host mouth).
- Arn: reedy, disdainful, aristocratic.
- NO narrator anywhere; only characters on screen speak.

## Shot list

### Act 0 — Frame (Earth, golden hour)
- **f1** — Aerial. A red-tailed hawk rides a thermal over a hidden Sierra valley. (silent)
- **f2** — The hawk lands by JARA: "Come, Tobias. Sit. I tell you story. Story of
  before. Story of Dak Hamee and Aldrea."
- **f3** — TITLE CARD over the alien valley: "THE HORK-BAJIR CHRONICLES".

### Act 1 — Exile
- **a1** — An Andalite dome ship descends over the colossal trees. (silent)
- **a2** — Dome interior. ALDREA: "You were a prince, father. Now they send you to
  watch trees grow at the edge of nowhere." SEEROW: "I gave the Yeerks the stars,
  Aldrea. Kindness was my crime. We will not speak of it again."
- **a3** — Aldrea gallops along the ridge, stalk eyes sweeping the valley. (silent)

### Act 2 — Dak
- **b1** — Valley life: Hork-Bajir peacefully stripping bark, families on branches. (silent)
- **b2** — Dak draws star-circles in bark dust. JAGIL: "Dak Hamee make shapes. Why
  make shapes?" DAK: "It is the sky, Jagil. The lights move. I watched. They move in circles."
- **b3** — ELDER: "Dak Hamee is seer. Born one time in all times. Seer come when
  change come."
- **b4** — Aldrea meets Dak on a high branch. DAK: "You are not of the trees. Not of
  the valley." ALDREA: "No. I am Aldrea. I come from the sky you watch."
- **b5** — Aldrea's holographic star map spins between them. ALDREA: "That light is
  not a hole in the sky, Dak. It is a sun. Like yours. And around it, worlds."
  DAK: "Worlds. Dak wants to see."
- **b6** — Dak teaches Aldrea branch-leaping; she nearly falls. DAK (laughing):
  "Andalite falls like stone!" (warm beat)

### Act 3 — The Deep / The Arn
- **c1** — They descend below the mist. ALDREA: "My father's instruments found
  something below the Deep, Dak. Something old. Something alive."
- **c2** — A monster of the Deep lunges from the dark; they scramble away. (silent, tense)
- **c3** — The Arn city reveal, vast and jeweled. DAK: "What... is this place?"
- **c4** — THE ARN: "Hork-Bajir. On my doorstep. You were not made to ask questions."
- **c5** — THE ARN: "Long ago the sky burned. We made the trees to heal the air. And
  we made YOU to tend the trees. Gardeners. Tools. Nothing more."
- **c6** — Dak, hollowed. DAK: "I am not a people. I am a tool that learned to dream."
  ALDREA: "A tool cannot choose, Dak. You choose. That makes you a people."

### Act 4 — The Yeerks arrive
- **d1** — Yeerk bug fighters slide down through Mother Sky. (silent, dread)
- **d2** — Pool ship bridge. ESPLIN: "Scans show no weapons. No metal. No fire. Only
  bodies. Billions of strong, bladed bodies."
- **d3** — Night. Hork-Bajir led onto a pier over a freshly dug pool; one is forced
  to kneel toward the grey water. (silent horror, discreet)
- **d4** — DAK: "Jagil. You did not eat. You do not laugh. Where does Jagil go at
  night?" JAGIL (flat): "Jagil is here. Jagil is... better now."
- **d5** — Dracon fire rakes the dome at night. SEEROW: "Aldrea — RUN!" (dome burning)
- **d6** — Aldrea alone in the smoking ruin. ALDREA: "Father. Mother. Barafin. All of
  them. They are all of them dead."

### Act 5 — Resistance
- **e1** — ALDREA: "The Yeerks take bodies, Dak. They will take every Hork-Bajir in
  the world unless we fight." DAK: "Hork-Bajir do not fight. Hork-Bajir do not even
  have a word for war." ALDREA: "Then we will teach them one."
- **e2** — Aldrea presses her hand to Dak's chest; he goes calm. DAK: "What do you
  do?" ALDREA: "Borrowing. Watch."
- **e3** — MORPH: Aldrea flows from Andalite to Hork-Bajir — fur to leather, blades
  erupting. (signature VFX shot, silent)
- **e4** — Training the free Hork-Bajir. DAK: "Blades were for bark. Now blades must
  be for blades."
- **e5** — After a won ambush, Dak stands over a fallen Controller. DAK: "He was
  Hork-Bajir too. The Yeerk was in his head, but the blood is Hork-Bajir blood. What
  are we becoming?"
- **e6** — ESPLIN: "A resistance. Led by an Andalite. Interesting. Tell the
  Sub-Visser I want that blue body taken alive. Someday it will be mine."

### Act 6 — Alloran and the virus
- **g1** — Eight Andalite fighters land. ALLORAN: "Eight warriors. That is what the
  council sends. Eight, for a world already lost."
- **g2** — ALLORAN: "Your father's kindness armed the enemy, child. Sentiment is how
  wars are lost." ALDREA: "And what do you call eight million Hork-Bajir, War-Prince?
  Acceptable losses?"
- **g3** — Alloran raises a small vial of swirling light. ALLORAN: "If the Yeerks
  want Hork-Bajir bodies... we deny them the bodies."
- **g4** — Night heist: Dak and Aldrea steal the canister from the Andalite camp. (silent, tense)
- **g5** — Yeerk ambush in the branches; dracon fire; the canister spins away into
  the dark and shatters. Dak takes a burn to the side. (chaos)
- **g6** — Aftermath: silent valleys, drifting pale spores, empty branches, bark
  untended. (silent, devastation)

### Act 7 — Nothlit / last stand
- **h1** — Sunrise. DAK: "Aldrea. The sun rises. Your two hours..." ALDREA (as
  Hork-Bajir now, gently): "Passed long ago, Dak Hamee. I am Hork-Bajir now. Your
  people are my people. Your fight is my fight."
- **h2** — The remnant band hidden in the highest branches. DAK: "The Yeerks take the
  world. They do not take all. While one Hork-Bajir is free — Hork-Bajir are free."
- **h3** — ESPLIN watches the stars from the pool ship. ESPLIN: "Sub-Visser Esplin,
  nine-four-double-six. Next, the Andalite home world. Next — everything."

### Act 8 — Frame close (Earth, dusk)
- **i1** — JARA: "Dak and Aldrea fight long. Fight until end. Their kawatnoj — their
  children's children — still free."
- **i2** — A young Hork-Bajir steps forward. JARA: "This is Toby. Toby Hamee. Named
  for you, Tobias." TOBY: "I am a seer, like Dak. I see what others do not see. I
  see us free."
- **i3** — The hawk takes wing over the valley into the sunset. Fade out. (silent)
