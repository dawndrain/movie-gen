# WALTER'S DEAL — storyboard (first pass, fast mode)

Adapted from *Walter's Deal* by Philipp Lehner (muddasheep, 2016) — a 379-page
retro-90s Faustian teleportation satire. This cut keeps the Faust spine and drops
the thief/Reklur B-plot:

> Walter, a basement scientist, invents teleportation. Xave Stern, a salesman,
> sells it to the world. The machine quietly kills its users (they don't decay,
> their insides knot, the first traveller nearly dies), every disaster is
> converted into a product ("The Time Machine" body-restore), and the two
> founders — who never dare use it themselves — end up in a corporate cemetery
> that mines graves for teleport-matter, wondering aloud where it will all end.

Target: ~36 shots, ~5 min, 480p **fast** (draft pass per MOVIE_LESSONS.md).
Dialogue carries everything; no narrator. Comedy dry, horror concentrated,
sincerity saved for the Kristella resurrection and the final scene.

## Eras / wardrobe locks

- **ERA1 (basement & warehouse, late-1980s Europe):** Walter in white coat, Xave in gray suit + red tie. Handmade tech, tube TVs, no modern devices.
- **ERA2 (+2 yrs, INSTANT):** white sterile temple-stations, blue INSTANT lettering, guards in futuristic blue uniforms.
- **ERA3 (+6 yrs):** Walter the bearded recluse; HQ is a converted hotel.
- **ERA4 (+12 yrs, epilogue):** gleaming corporate monolith; Walter clean and gray-templed; blue-glowing cemetery under a black tent.

## Voice locks (every dialogue shot)

- WALTER: light precise tenor, mild Central-European accent, hesitant and dry.
- XAVE: smooth confident baritone, quick, theatrical salesman patter.
- KRISTELLA: warm wry alto.
- Always: "There is no narrator and no voiceover; only characters visible on
  screen speak, lips moving."
