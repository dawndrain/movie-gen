## Setting descriptions (for Nano Banana frame prompts)

- **Beaulieu chapter house**: broad lofty room, oaken floor, long oaken benches
  lining walls, carved high chairs, rows of white-robed Cistercian monks,
  candle-light, a carved oak prie-dieu center-stage.
- **Beaulieu farewell view**: "the Abbot's house, the long church, the cloisters
  with their line of arches, all bathed and mellowed in the evening sun."
- **New Forest (autumn)**: "sun shining slantwise through the trees threw delicate
  traceries across the road, with bars of golden light between"; beeches turning
  coppery red; amber peaty streams; giant oaks "the majestic arches of Nature's
  own cathedral" over "a broad carpet of the softest and greenest moss."
- **Pied Merlin inn, exterior**: long low beam-built thatched house, two flambeaux
  blazing by the door, ale-pole with greenery, painted wooden heraldic shields
  under the eaves. **Interior**: smoke-blackened low ceiling, huge open fire with
  bubbling cauldron, benches sunk in clay floor, resinous torches, "red light and
  black shadow."
- **Minstead manor clearing**: broad green lawn with cows and black swine, brown
  forest stream with a rude bridge, long low thatched wooden house with open
  window-squares, blue smoke through a hole in the thatch, great black hound
  chained at the door-post.
- **Twynham Castle**: black gateway with torches casting red glare, screeching
  drawbridge and rattling portcullis, grass-sown baileys, gaunt windowless square
  keep on a mound; great hall with log fire, bracket-lamps, Sir Bevis tapestries,
  black buffet with gold cups. Pre-dawn muster: torchlight circles, bowmen ten
  deep, steel horsemen statue-still, women sobbing at the light's edge.
- **The yellow cog**: "high ended, deep waisted, painted of a canary yellow, and
  towering above the fishing-boats like a swan among ducklings"; broad purple
  mainsail with "a golden St. Christopher bearing Christ upon his shoulder";
  horse stalls in the waist, mantlets along the bulwarks.
- **Pirate galleys**: "long, black and swift... like two fierce lean wolves";
  double oar-banks, mangonels on forecastles; Norman sail badged with an
  Ethiop's head, Genoese with the red cross.
- **Bordeaux**: "forest of masts... the gray crescent-shaped city with many a
  tower and minaret along the western shore"; smoke of steel forges; royal
  banner over the Abbey of St. Andrew. Prince's room: dais under "a broad canopy
  of scarlet velvet spangled with silver fleurs-de-lis," two star-powdered
  blue-silk thrones, the Prince on a plain stool BEFORE them, hooded gerfalcons
  on a perch.
- **The lists**: "a dark sea of heads"; green strip marked with banners; knights'
  pavilions flying the martlets of Audley, roses of Loring, scarlet bars of Wake,
  Percy lion, Beauchamp silver wings; beyond, "the broad, white, empty highway"
  over bare winter vineyards.
- **Ravaged France**: "black blotches of burned farm-steadings... gray, gaunt
  gable-ends of what had been chateaux. Broken fences, crumbling walls, vineyards
  littered with stones, the shattered arches of bridges"; brushwood hovels "more
  like a fowl's nest than the dwelling-place of man."
- **Villefranche**: "A broad moat, a high outer wall turreted at the corners,
  with a great black keep towering above all — so it lay before them in the
  moonlight." Feast hall: pasty-laden board, hooded hawks on perches, deer-hounds
  on tiles, lilac-coated pages — ragged watchers outside staring at "the golden
  bar of light." Burning bailey: "great yellow sheets of flame," looted silk
  gowns on peasant shoulders, brushwood piled at the walls; the keep summit "a
  last island of refuge" over "a sea of fire."
- **Roncesvalles**: dawn, rosy snow-capped peaks, shadowed cliffs both sides,
  knee-deep snow, "the far-stretching, unbroken river of steel," breath "reeking
  up upon the frosty air like the steam from a cauldron."
- **Spanish camp before Burgos**: "a great city of tents — thousands upon
  thousands... laid out in streets and in squares"; center "a huge palace of red
  and white silk, with the royal arms of Castile."
- **The last-stand gorge**: March mist rolling; "one gigantic peak, with the pink
  glow of the early sunshine upon its snow-capped head" above the vapor;
  wedge-shaped cleft walled by brown crags; the hill — front slope jagged and
  boulder-strewn, rear a sheer 100+ ft cliff, summit plateau ~100×50 paces.
  Mist-parting reveal: "tossing pennons, twinkling lances, waving plumes...
  glittering, many-colored mass," Moorish cymbals.
- **Romsey nunnery procession**: flag-and-flower streets, gold crucifix,
  incense-bearers, twenty-two white-clad flower-casting singers, the "dank, cold
  air... from the black arch" of the church door vs. sun and birdsong outside.
- **Final image**: "the dark keep of Twynham towered above them in the gloaming,
  and they saw the red sun lying athwart the rippling Avon."

## Character verbatim (top-up beyond storyboard anchor table)

- Aylward: "his shaven face was as brown as a hazel-nut... a long white scar
  which stretched from the corner of his left nostril to the angle of the jaw";
  kissed the ground at Hythe; illiterate; "quick to love, and quick to hate."
- Nigel: "a slight man of poor stature, with soft lisping voice and gentle ways";
  age 46; "a pate which was as bald as an egg"; partly shorn left ear hidden by
  his hat; 27 wounds; near-sighted (lime at Bergerac); war-horse Pommers;
  in battle "as if the devil was in him... so swift and so erratic."
- John: "his huge body, with his great muscles swelling out like the gnarled
  roots of an oak"; 270 pounds; voice "like the flap of a sail."
- Maude: "tall and slight and dark... her jet-black hair was gathered back under
  a light pink coif... her step long and springy, like that of some wild,
  tireless woodland creature"; "a woman's body, with a soul of fire"; falcon
  named Roland.
- Chandos: "hair... white as the new-fallen snow... the loss of one eye which
  had been torn from the socket"; "the look of some fierce old bird of prey";
  wears the long linen parement "destined to be the cause of his death."
- Du Guesclin: "the eyes were of the lightest green, the nose was broken and
  driven inwards"; voice "as deep and as fierce as the growl of a beast of
  prey"; war-cry "Notre Dame Du Guesclin! St. Ives! St. Ives!"
- Tiphaine in trance: cheeks blanch lily-white, eyes fix on the wall, voice "low
  and muffled as that of one who speaks from a distance"; carries a
  silver-hilted poniard.
- Black Prince: "a slim, dark young man, whose quiet attire and modest manner
  would scarce proclaim him to be the most noted prince in Europe."
- Don Pedro: "a very tall and well formed man with red hair, a livid face, and a
  cold blue eye... something peculiarly sinister"; fondles a Spanish greyhound.
