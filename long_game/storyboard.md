# The Long Game — Storyboard & Production Plan

Working format: **16:9**, short film ~10–12 min from ~45 shots (Seedance clips of 5–15s each).
Narration-driven: the story's voice is its best asset, so plan on **voiceover narration** carrying
most shots, with sparse on-screen dialogue (Seedance 2.0 does lip-sync when the prompt includes
spoken lines).

## Visual style bible

One consistent cinematic language, but each era gets its own grade so the audience always knows
where (when) we are:

| Era | Palette / look |
|---|---|
| 2044 Arcade | Neon magenta/cyan on deep black, clean optimized future, shallow DOF, slight anamorphic flare |
| Bronze Age | Cold blue-grey dawn, mud and smoke, handheld feel, desaturated except firelight |
| Roman | Warm terracotta and marble white, hard Mediterranean sun, steam and bronze |
| Renaissance | Ink black and paper cream, candlelit interiors, Vermeer window light |
| 1926 | Deco retrofuturism (locked: street_v3 look). 1920s fashion is sacred — cloche hats, drop-waist dresses, three-piece suits — but the city runs on near-future tech: elevated glass transit tubes, sleek modern electric vehicles (NOT vintage cars), deco-typography electric signage, radio spires, brilliant steady light, prosperous and spotless. Warm color film look, never sepia-monochrome. No year numerals anywhere |
| Arcade again | Identical to 2044 but subtly *too* clean — half a step uncanny |

Recurring visual motifs to keep in prompts:
- **The hand circling over water** (the greeting) — appears in Bronze, Roman, and echoes in the final code gesture
- **The touched woman** — same actress-face in every era, different dress
- **White-out transitions** between lives
- **Steady vs. flickering light** — flame in old eras, unwavering electric light in 1926/2044

## Character anchors (generate these FIRST, reuse as `--image` refs everywhere)

1. **Cass @ 17** — white teenager, lean, watchful, quiet intensity; reads everything around him
2. **Cass aged** — same face across the lives: **~25–40** Bronze (rises to king ~25, tired king by 40), **40–50** Roman engineer, **50–60** Renaissance printer, **60–80** schoolmaster/widower. Generate ~5 age anchors (17, 28, 45, 55, 70) by feeding the age-17 anchor + "aged to N" prompts; push 70 → 80 in-prompt when needed
3. **Deshawn** — tall, expressive, magnetic, always mid-laugh
4. **Milo** — compact, glasses-coded even in 2044, the one who reads the docs
5. **The touched woman** — ageless weathered face, eyes that focus for exactly one sentence
6. **Iris** — 52, practical, warm, merciless about doormats
7. **Faustus** — Roman glassblower, quick warm funny, Cass's equal
8. Location plates: arcade retro floor, the Deep End cabinet, bronze village, Roman forum/baths, print shop, 1926 boarding house & library reading room

## Production order

1. **Anchors** (~15 images @ 1k): characters + key locations. Iterate until faces/looks are right.
2. **Storyboard frames** (~44 images @ 1k, using anchors as refs): one keyframe per shot above. Cheap to redo.
3. **Review pass** — reorder/cut shots while they're still images.
4. **Video** (480p fast to draft, re-render keepers at 720p/1080p): storyboard frame as `--start-image`, character refs as `--image`, prompt = motion + camera + audio/dialogue.
5. **Narration + stitch**: record/generate VO, assemble with ffmpeg.

Cost ballpark: images are cheap (test was fine on credits); 44 draft clips at 480p fast ≈ 7.5 credits × 44 ≈ **330 credits** for a full video draft pass, well within the ~1000 balance.
